<p><p>Waterborne polyurethane resin for synthetic leather can be categorized into dry and wet processes. The dry process involves applying the resin onto a substrate and allowing it to evaporate, resulting in a flexible, durable surface. This method is efficient and environmentally friendly due to lower solvent emissions. In contrast, the wet process involves mixing the resin with water and other additives, which is then coated on the substrate; this provides enhanced adhesion and texture. Both methods cater to diverse synthetic leather applications.</p></p>
