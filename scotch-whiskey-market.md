<p><p>The Scotch whisky market encompasses three main types: Pure Malt Whisky, Grain Whisky, and Blended Whisky. Pure Malt Whisky is made exclusively from malted barley in pot stills, showcasing rich flavors and complexity. Grain Whisky is produced from grains other than barley, often in column stills, resulting in a lighter, more neutral taste. Blended Whisky combines both malt and grain whiskies, balancing the characteristics of each to achieve a smooth and accessible flavor profile, appealing to a wide audience.</p></p>
